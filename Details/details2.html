More Informations

Like thyme, rosemary and savory, marjoram belongs to the mint family. It is a spice plant that comes from the Mediterranean and can therefore also be found in many Mediterranean dishes. The marjoram is a cold-sensitive perennial herb or undershrub with sweet pine and citrus flavors, grows in the vast desert nature of Tunisia. In some Middle Eastern countries, marjoram is synonymous with oregano, and there the names sweet marjoram and knotted marjoram are used to distinguish it from other plants of the genus Origanum. It is also called pot marjoram, although this name is also used for other cultivated species of Origanum. The spice is also used to refine sausages, which is why it is also known as "sausage herb".
