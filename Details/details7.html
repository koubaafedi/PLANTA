Carob (Ceratonia siliqua)

More Informations

The carob tree has been grown since antiquity in most countries of the Mediterranean basin (Tunisia), usually in mild and dry places with poor soils. Its value was recognized by the ancient Greeks, who brought it from its native Middle East to Greece and Italy, and by the Arabs, who disseminated it along the North African coast and north into Spain and Portugal.

It grows well in warm temperate and subtropical areas, tolerates hot and humid coastal areas.
