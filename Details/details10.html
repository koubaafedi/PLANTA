Boswellia serrata (frankincense)

Frankincense is a resin that escapes from the bark of the frankincense tree when injured.

How is frankincense actually made?

Frankincense is a resin that escapes from the bark of the frankincense tree when injured. It serves to seal wounds and protect the tree from infection. To obtain frankincense resin, the trees are scratched with a knife. Small drops emerge that dry in the air.

Sacrifice and Sacred Gift

In ancient Rome, incense was highly valued as an offering. And even shortly after the birth of Jesus it plays a central role: He received incense as a gift from one of the three kings.

Do it like the ancient Egyptians did - make your own incense ointment

If you want to buy frankincense and love natural cosmetics, try a homemade frankincense ointment: Make a powder from 10 g frankincense and gently heat it with 40 g almond oil or jojoba oil. Then let it steep for two to three hours and strain through a coffee filter. Now you can add other oils like evening primrose oil, olive oil or sesame oil to the frankincense oil. Mix everything well and then heat it in a water bath along with about 7g of beeswax. Pour this natural treasure into a glass and pamper your skin with it when it needs a soothing break.

Alternate names

Frankincense is also known as boswellia resin, incens, frankincens, lalai guggal, and bakhour, among others.
