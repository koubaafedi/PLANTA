Lavender originally comes from the Mediterranean region such as If you have ever been to the north west of Tunisiayou will surely have passed one of the vast lavender fields. The herbaceous plants grow there in huge fields and exude an intoxicating scent. From June to September, its purple flowers bathe the whole country in a dreamy, romantic light. And when the flower comes to an end, many Tunisian people start filling lavender bags. These small pillow-like sachets are used as scented sachets, giving off a stunning scent that you can relax and enjoy anywhere in the house, even after the season is overIts blue-purple flowers are not only pretty to look at, they can also be used in many ways. In ancient Rome, lavender was often used as a bath additive or for washing clothes because of its intense scent. In the 12th century, real lavender (Lavandula angustifolia) was made known as a medicinal plant by Hildegard von Bingen. In the cosmetics industry, lavender is often used as an additive in care products and perfumes because of its intense fragrance.

MediPlanta® lavender blossoms come from controlled cultivation and are gently dried after harvesting so that the aroma-typical fragrances are preserved. The flowers are completely natural and are therefore suitable for the production of scented pillows, scented potpourries and intensely scented soap.

Our MediPlanta® lavender blossoms are free from artificial fragrances and are available in the usual premium quality.
